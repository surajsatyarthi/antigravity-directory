## Competitor Profiles

### 1. Antigravity Awesome Skills

- **NAME AND URL:** Antigravity Awesome Skills (https://antigravityskills.org). Validated via their associated GitHub repository (`sickn33/antigravity-awesome-skills`).
- **LAUNCH DATE:** Launched between late 2025 and early 2026, giving them roughly a 2-3 month head start since Antigravity's initial release.
- **CONTENT DEPTH:** The web platform visually curates 244+ agentic skills broken down into 10 categories (Coding, DevOps, Security, Marketing, etc.). Their backing GitHub registry claims over 968+ skills.
- **TRAFFIC ESTIMATE:** Massive developer interest. Their backing GitHub repository boasts a staggering **17.8k stars** and 3.2k forks, placing it among the most popular early AI agent registries for Antigravity.
- **MONETISATION MODEL:** Purely free and open-source. There is no visible revenue model, sponsorships, or paid listings on the site or repository.
- **THEIR WEAKNESSES:** They are heavily over-indexed technically on "Skills" (executable bundle packages installed via CLI) rather than lightweight configurations. They lack simple, isolated copy-paste global rules (`GEMINI.md`) and pre-configured MCP server connections. Furthermore, their installation loop relies heavily on terminal commands (`npx antigravity-awesome-skills`) rather than a simple, friction-free web UI.
- **THEIR STRENGTHS:** Exceptional content depth and categorical organization. They have a massive first-mover community advantage with extreme social proof (17.8k stars). Their programmatic CLI installer creates strong developer stickiness once adopted.

### 2. Awesome Antigravity

- **NAME AND URL:** Awesome Antigravity (https://awesome-antigravity.com). Validated via their associated GitHub repository (`ZhangYu-zjut/awesome-Antigravity`).
- **LAUNCH DATE:** Launched between late 2025 and early 2026.
- **CONTENT DEPTH:** Comparatively shallow. The directory lists roughly 100+ basic prompts, agent personas, and mission control templates.
- **TRAFFIC ESTIMATE:** Much lower traffic and footprint. The backing GitHub repository has only **107 stars** and 10 forks.
- **MONETISATION MODEL:** Free, community-driven directory. No visible monetization.
- **THEIR WEAKNESSES:** Very low social proof and traction compared to the `sickn33` repository. The content is shallow, acting as a basic prompt-dump rather than providing executable workflows, advanced `.antigravity/rules.md` logic, or MCP integrations. They lack any filtering by category or advanced programmatic deployment.
- **THEIR STRENGTHS:** Excellent "One-Click Copy" user experience. They focus on low-friction, easy-to-understand prompts that immediately appeal to beginners who don't want to use CLI installers.

### 3. Antigravity AI Directory

- **NAME AND URL:** Antigravity AI Directory (https://antigravityai.directory).
- **LAUNCH DATE:** Late 2025 / Early 2026.
- **CONTENT DEPTH:** High. Features 125+ specialized prompts and over 430 preconfigured MCP integrations covering services like AWS, Stripe, GitHub, and Supabase.
- **TRAFFIC ESTIMATE:** Strong organic presence and active community resource. No public exact star count available, but it is heavily referenced as a primary web hub.
- **MONETISATION MODEL:** Appears to be a free community resource with no explicit paywalls or paid listings yet.
- **THEIR WEAKNESSES:** The sheer volume of content is presented in massive, text-heavy categorical blocks which could overwhelm users without a top-tier semantic search interface. They lack a highly visible open-source GitHub repository for immediate community-driven pull requests.
- **THEIR STRENGTHS:** Enormous volume of out-of-the-box MCP server configurations. They provide instant "copy to clipboard" functionality and deep, exhaustive categorization covering frameworks from React to Rust.

### 4. Antigravity Codes

- **NAME AND URL:** Antigravity Codes (https://antigravity.codes).
- **LAUNCH DATE:** January 2026.
- **CONTENT DEPTH:** Massive claims. They advertise 1,500+ MCP servers and 500+ AI coding rules and workflows.
- **TRAFFIC ESTIMATE:** Likely very high, as they have positioned themselves as a cross-IDE platform (targeting Cursor, Windsurf, and Antigravity users simultaneously).
- **MONETISATION MODEL:** Free resources with lead generation mechanics (e.g., opting in to "Get 850+ Gemini 3 Coding Prompts").
- **THEIR WEAKNESSES:** By targeting three different AI IDEs (Cursor, Windsurf, Antigravity), their content is heavily diluted. Many of their rules are likely generic AI prompts rather than deeply optimized, Antigravity-native configurations (like specific `GEMINI.md` syntax or subagent routing).
- **THEIR STRENGTHS:** Unmatched scale in sheer numbers. They capture a massive organic top-of-funnel by targeting users of multiple AI code editors and providing comprehensive beginner tutorials and troubleshooting guides alongside their directory.

## Our exploitable advantages over all competitors combined

- **The "Antigravity-Native" Focus:** Unlike `antigravity.codes` which dilutes its focus across Cursor and Windsurf, we will be 100% laser-focused on Antigravity's unique features, such as deep browser-subagent configuration, specific `GEMINI.md` guidelines, and agent orchestration—not just generic prompt-dumps.
- **Unified Ecosystem vs Fragmentation:** Competitor 1 only targets CLI "Skills," Competitor 2 targets lightweight "Prompts", and Competitor 3 provides massive text walls. We will be the first to aggregate _everything_ into a clean, highly searchable hub with seamless UX: Prompts, Rules, Skills, and MCP Server configs.
- **Better Search & Discovery:** The existing directories with deep content (like `antigravityai.directory` and `antigravity.codes`) suffer from content bloat. Implementing robust filtering, semantic search, and tagging will make finding the exact required MCP server exponentially faster.
- **Better Monetisation Strategy:** The competitors are either purely non-profit or using basic lead gen. We can introduce a sustainable model (sponsorships, premium featured tool listings for SaaS companies wanting their MCP servers highlighted) immediately, allowing us to outspend them on curation and SEO growth.
- **The UX Sweet Spot:** We will merge the deep, professional organization of `antigravityskills.org` with the dead-simple "One-Click Copy" UI of `awesome-antigravity.com`, instantly serving both advanced and beginner engineers.

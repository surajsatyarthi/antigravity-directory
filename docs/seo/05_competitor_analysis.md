# Competitor Analysis

## Primary Competitor — antigravity.codes

### Overview
Direct competitor. Already operating. Claims "#1 directory for AI IDE developers."
Covers Google Antigravity + Cursor + Windsurf (multi-IDE strategy).

### Their Strengths
- Blog content with original articles (critical for SEO)
- 500+ coding rules
- 50+ workflows
- Community forum
- Lead magnet: "850+ Gemini 3 Coding Prompts" PDF
- Multi-IDE positioning (3x our addressable market)
- Established before us

### Their Weaknesses
- Fewer MCP servers (1,500 vs our 2,215)
- No explicit job board
- Less total resources than our directory

### What We Must Do Better
1. More original content (blog articles)
2. Better rules section (currently 45, they have 500+)
3. Add multi-IDE coverage (Cursor, Windsurf resources)
4. Build a lead magnet (PDF download to capture emails)

## Benchmark — cursor.directory

### Why It Matters
Same business model on a comparable product (Cursor IDE).
Proves the model works. Proves the revenue potential.

### Key Stats
- Revenue: $35,000/month (self-reported, verified)
- Founder: Solo, Sweden, side project
- Founded: 2024
- Model: Directory + job board + sponsored listings + newsletter

### Revenue Breakdown (Estimated)
- Job board listings: $99—$299 per listing
- Sponsored slots: $300—$2,000/month
- Newsletter: per-send sponsor fees
- Total: $35,000/month at scale

### How They Got Traffic
- Cursor IDE became hugely popular among developers
- cursor.directory ranked for "cursor rules", "cursor mcp", "cursor prompts"
- Job board created a network effect (developers visit to find jobs, companies visit to post)

### What We Replicate
1. Same directory structure ✓ (already done)
2. Sponsored listings ✓ (already set up)
3. SEO content targeting high volume keywords (in progress)
4. Job board (launch when traffic hits 10,000/month)
5. Newsletter (already have signup)
